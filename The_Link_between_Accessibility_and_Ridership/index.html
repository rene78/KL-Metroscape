The Link between Accessibility and Ridership

A closer look at the low passenger numbers of Kuala Lumpur's MRT lines

Abstract

This article aims to investigate the factors contributing to the underwhelming ridership of Kuala Lumpur's Mass Rapid Transport (MRT) system. The primary issues identified include station inaccessibility, insufficient first and last-mile connectivity, and a notable absence of infrastructure supporting pedestrian and human-powered modes of transportation. In this context, the article not only presents overarching concepts for improving the public transportation system, but also provides specific and cost-effective recommendations for the MRT Putrajaya Line that could lead to a significant increase in ridership.

Introduction

In 2010 it was decided by the Malaysian government to implement a state-of-the-art Mass Rapid Transit (MRT) for Kuala Lumpur to achieve the following goals:

  • Efficient Mobility: Provide fast and efficient transportation, reducing the ever worsening traffic congestion problem in Klang Valley.
  • Reduced Dependency on Cars: Encourage a shift from private car usage, saving energy and reducing reliance on fossil fuels.
  • Environmental Sustainability: Promote eco-friendly transportation, reducing air pollution and greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Economic Growth: Attract investments, stimulate economic activity, and enhance property values along their routes.
  • Increased inclusion: Give more freedom to individuals with limited physical capabilities or financial resources.
  • Quality of Life: Improves overall quality of life by reducing stress, noise, congestion and pollution.
As of 2023 two lines - the Kajang Line and Putrajaya Line - have been constructed for a total cost of RM51.5 billion. Together with a third line - the Circle Line - which is being constructed for another RM50 billion, this represents the largest infrastructure project ever undertaken in Malaysia.
